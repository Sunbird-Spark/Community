# Platform in 30 minutes

_For technical architects, product managers, and senior developers who want to understand the full platform before committing to adoption._

This guide walks you through Sunbird Spark from three role perspectives.

***

**As a Learner&#x20;**_**(10 minutes)**_

**1. Browse without an account**

Visit the Spark portal. Without logging in, you can:

* View the homepage with curated content sections (Most Popular, Trending, Resource Centre)
* Browse content by category
* Open and consume individual content items (PDF, video, interactive)
* Use the search bar and apply filters (Content Type, Category, Collections)

{% hint style="info" %}
This is the anonymous consumption experience. Any content marked as "public" by your administrators is accessible without a login.
{% endhint %}

**2. Sign up and explore your dashboard**

Register for an account. After registering you will see:

* A personalised home dashboard with four summary tiles: **Total Courses**, **In Progress**, **Completed**, **Certifications Earned**
* A _Continue where you left off_ widget (once you have started a course)
* A _Recommended for you_ section

**3. Enrol in a course and track your progress**

Find a course in the Explore section and click **Enrol**. Navigate to **My Learning** to see:

* Ongoing, completed, and upcoming courses in separate tabs
* Visual progress bars per course
* A Learning Progress widget showing lessons visited and content completed

**4. Complete a course and download your certificate**

Complete all lessons in a course. Once the completion threshold is met:

* A certificate is automatically generated
* The certificate appears in your Profile page.
* You can download it as a PDF
* You can share your certificate with employers or institutions for verification using QR (Already present in the downloaded certificate)

***

**As a Content Creator&#x20;**_**(10 minutes)**_

**1. Access the workspace**

Log in with a Content Creator account. In the left sidebar, navigate to the workspace (the pencil/content icon). You will see:

* **All** — all content you have created, with status (Draft, In Review, Live, Retired)
* **More > Uploads** — directly uploaded content items
* **More > Collaborations** — content shared with you by others

**2. Create a course**

Click **Create** and choose **Course**. In the Collection Editor:

* Give the course a name & description
* Add units (sections) and lessons
* Upload or link content items to each lesson
* Add a QuML-based assessment at the end

**3. Submit for review**

When the course is ready, click **Submit for Review**. The course moves to the Content Reviewer's queue.

**4. Publish&#x20;**_**(as a reviewer)**_

Log in with a Content Reviewer account. In the workspace, navigate to **Up for Review**. Open the course, review each lesson, then click **Publish**. The course is now live and discoverable by learners.

***

**As an Administrator&#x20;**_**(10 minutes)**_

**1. View platform reports**

Log in with an Admin account. In the left sidebar, click **Admin Reports**. You will see:

* **Content Overview** — content by status (Draft / Live / Retired), content by taxonomy grouping, top contributors
* **Admin Course Summary** — searchable table with completion percentages (colour-coded) and Export CSV
* **User Analytics** — total users, growth trend

**2. Create a course batch** _(Content Creator or Course Admin only)_

Navigate to **Explore Page** and find a published course. Go to **Manage Batches**, then create a new batch:

* Set the batch name, start date, and enrolment end date
* Set the certificate threshold (e.g., 100% completion triggers certificate issuance)
* Set enrolment to open

**3. Manage users**

Navigate to **User Management**. Search for a user by their Sunbird ID or email. Assign or remove roles. View their consent status.
